Hades Data Mining

All Data comes from Gamepedia

Hades) is a roguelike game from Supergiant Games, creators of Bastion, Transistor, and Pyre. You play as Zagreus, immortal son of Hades, on his quest to escape from the underworld, fighting through many angry lost souls along the way. Death is a mere inconvenience for Zagreus, returning to his room in Hades’ palace each time to reflect in the Mirror of Night and try again. Receive Boons from the other gods of Olympus and find treasures to aid in each escape attempt.

As for game itself, it is an ideal project to practice data mining. As you see, various type of combination of boons can be made during the gaming more complicated. My goal is to figure a way to decide which boon to select in order to make a way out of Hell.

📣 Intro

There are a few prerequisites on how Boons work that need to be clear to everyone. These are not immediately obvious or self explanatory, so please read through them before moving on.

When reading a Boon, there are two things you can look out for:

  • You might see numbers in parenthesis. This denotes that the number is part of a multiplier as opposed to a flat damage number. Here’s an example: 50% x 1.3 = 65%, so the damage increase will be 65%!
  • You might see an asterisk (*) before the name. This denotes there being something of note to do with the Boon, such as an interaction between itself and another Boon.

Boons come in six Rarities: Common, Rare, Epic, Heroic are the main four we’ll worry about. Boons do not come with a set rarity, and thus will have four different sets of numbers. We’ll be denoting these 4 rarities like this throughout the guide: C for common, R for Rare, E for Epic:,H for Heroic.

The fifth rarity is called Legendary, which are modifiers available to a specific Boon on each God. These Boons will be outlined and bold faced at the bottom of every God’s page, like so.

Finally, the sixth rarity, Duo, are unique in that they will not appear unless you have specific Boons from two different Gods. Those will be included in their own section as opposed to Boons for the individual gods.

Boon comes from Gods of Olympics. 9 Gods provides different types of boons that focus on different style of fight. Your job is to select or pray for the one that fits you the best to balance survival between damage to foes.

🌓Chaos

Chaos’ Boons work differently from all others. Every boon has two parts, a Blessing and a Curse.

Curses are demerits that occur for a few Encounters. They do not tick down for rooms that do not have Enemies, such as Charon shops or NPC rooms. They are always temporary and will go away after the set number of encounters is cleared.

Blessings are permanent positive effects that are only gained after the Curse is removed, meaning you must go through the required number of Encounters to gain the benefits of the Blessing.

These Blessings and Curses are in two separate categories and any Blessing can be matched with any Curse, making for a HUGE variety in match ups. As such, I will only be going over their effects and not the specific numbers behind each one for the time being.

Duo

Duo Boons are a type of Boon which combines the powers of two different gods for a unique effect. Duo Boons can only be obtained with the correct prerequisite boons and, like normal boons, are lost when Zagreus dies and returns to the House of Hades.

There are currently 28 Duo Boons in Hades, shared between 8 different gods: Aphrodite, Ares, Artemis, Athena, Dionysus, Demeter, Poseidon, and Zeus. Hermes and Chaos will never offer Duo Boons. Every god that may have Duo boons shares one with each other god.Duo boons require you to have two specific Boons in order to be available from the God. They do not come with differing stats based on rarity, and the Boons can be a variety of things to meet the requirements of the Duo Boon. As such, in place of the rarity section, it will be a list of Boons that will work for unlocking the Duo Boon.
